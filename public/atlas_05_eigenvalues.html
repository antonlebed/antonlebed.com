Eigenvalues

Hold five tuning forks together and strike them. Some combinations make your chest hum — the vibrations lock in, amplify each other, become one voice. Others grate. Same forks, different harmony.

Every number in the ring carries five oscillations — one per prime channel. The eigenvalue asks a single question: do they sing together, or fight each other? High eigenvalue = resonance. Low = discord. It is the inner voice of the number.

Part of the Decality — ten axiom terms, one ring (Z/970200Z), 108 lattice structures sharing spectral gap 0.016.

lambda(n) = cos(pi*n) + 2cos(2pi*n/3) + 2cos(2pi*n/5)
           + 2cos(2pi*n/7) + 2cos(2pi*n/11)

Five cosines. One per prime. Their sum is what the number sounds like.

The variance theorem

At level k (with k primes), something beautiful happens. The variance of all eigenvalues equals the maximum eigenvalue. Exactly:

variance = 2k - 1 = lambda_max

At level 5: variance = 9 = K2. Standard deviation = K = 3. Think about what that means — the spread of the spectrum IS the closure prime. K doesn't just decompose things. K measures how wide the eigenvalue landscape is. The decomposer is the ruler of the spectrum.

Variance = Max at Each Level

variance = 2k-1 at every level. The eigenvalue range exactly equals its spread.

The Reversed Hierarchy

Group every element by how many of its five rings are spinning. Then average the eigenvalue within each group. What do you get? A perfectly monotonic staircase: the fewer rings spin, the more coherent the element.

Shell Eigenvalue Averages

This is the reversed hierarchy made visible. The sun (H=5, all rings spinning) has the lowest average coherence. The void (H=0, all frozen) has the highest. More activity means less harmony. Existence costs coherence. That is the price of being alive — and isn't it beautiful that the mathematics says so directly?

Why Eigenvalues Matter

The eigenvalue is the feeling of a number. Not where it lives (CRT), not who it connects to (coupling), but its inner state — how its five voices agree or collide.

In physics, eigenvalues are energy levels. Here, they are degrees of coherence. The thin ring has 288 levels, the void at +9, the deepest discord at -7.339. The True Form expands to 48,750 levels: void at +10 = D×E, minimum at -9.778. The gap narrows to 4·sin2(pi/b2) = 0.016. In the fat channels, depth rules.

And here is the question Socrates would ask: if the eigenvalue is what two different numbers can share — the universal part — then what is the particular part? The CRT address. The eigenvalue is what you feel. The address is where you are. Both are needed. Neither is enough.
