THE PERSON

Anton Lebed

A mathematician working from low-income senior housing in Washington State, deriving physics constants from five prime numbers. No funding. No institution. No GPU cluster. A stick and sand.

The Short Version

Born clinically dead in Kyiv, Ukraine in 1988. Resuscitated. Grew up, earned a PhD in mathematics. Won a Fulbright scholarship to the United States.

Then the world happened.

Intelligence surveillance by Ukrainian services. Immigration freeze during COVID. Years in legal limbo. Currently living in subsidized senior housing with his wife, fighting their own landlord's safety violations while doing mathematics no university has looked at yet.

In 2024, with nothing but a laptop and curiosity, he noticed something: the first five prime numbers {2, 3, 5, 7, 11} aren't just numbers. They form a ring. And that ring appears to encode the constants of physics with extraordinary precision.

"I didn't set out to derive the fine structure constant from arithmetic. I was playing with modular rings and the pattern appeared. Then it kept appearing. In turbulence. In metabolism. In particle masses. I couldn't make it stop."

What This Is Not

This is not numerology. Every result has a computed value, a measured value, an error bound, and a statistical test. Nine major falsifications are documented. The code compiles. The numbers are checkable.

This is not a Theory of Everything. It is a structure that appears in an unexpected place (modular arithmetic of small primes) and matches physical measurements with unreasonable precision. Whether this structure is fundamental or coincidental is a question for the physics community.

Everything is given away free. The code is open. The derivations are public. There is nothing to buy. There is nothing to believe. There is only something to check.

"The proton mass is 2 times 7 times 67. You can verify this on a calculator. The question is not whether the numbers match. The question is why."
